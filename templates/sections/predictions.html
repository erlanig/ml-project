Mammographic Mass Category

Mammography mass categories understand categories by grouping predictions into different levels: Benign, Malignant helps you predict mammography mass and understand the results.

1. Benign

Benign mammographic masses are non-cancerous abnormalities typically characterized by well-defined borders and a low likelihood of spreading to other tissues.

2. Malignant

Malignant mammographic masses are cancerous lesions that may exhibit irregular borders, rapid growth, and the potential to invade nearby tissues, posing a higher risk of metastasis.
